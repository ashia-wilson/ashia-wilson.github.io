I will be joining the MIT faculty as an assistant professor in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science (EECS). There, I will be a member of the Faculty of Artificial Intelligence and Decision-Making (AI&D) and the Laboratory for Information & Decisions Systems (LIDS). I obtained my BA from Harvard University with a concentration in applied mathematics and a minor in philosophy, and my PhD from UC Berkeley in statistics. Before joining MIT, I held a postdoctoral position in the machine learning group at Microsoft Research, New England. In my free time, I enjoy reading, listening to music, and taking long walks.

Research


My research focuses on the methodological foundations, theory, and fairness in machine learning. I am interested in developing frameworks for algorithmic assessment and providing rigorous guarantees for algorithmic performance in machine learning.
